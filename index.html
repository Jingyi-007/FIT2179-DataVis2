📊 Access vs Outcomes

Access vs. Success: Does More Schools Mean More Graduates?

States with more schools generally achieve higher completion rates.

Key Insights


Strong Performers
Sarawak and Sabah lead in school count while maintaining above 100% completion rates.


Urban Outliers
W.P. Kuala Lumpur and W.P. Labuan have lower completion rates despite being urban areas — suggesting access alone doesn't guarantee outcomes.


East vs West
East Malaysian states (Sabah, Sarawak) have more schools but lower population density, while West Malaysian states have higher enrolment per school.


The 100% Line
States above the red dotted line have achieved full primary completion. Most states exceed this threshold.

📊 Rankings

The Graduation Race: Who Leads the State Rankings?

Tracks how each state's primary completion rate ranking changed over time.

Key Insights


Consistently High
Perlis and Terengganu maintained top rankings throughout 2016–2022, reflecting stable and strong education outcomes.


Most Volatile
W.P. Putrajaya fell from Rank 1 in 2016 to Rank 14 in 2021 — the most dramatic drop in the dataset — before recovering in 2022.


Most Improved
Negeri Sembilan climbed from Rank 11 in 2016 to Rank 4 by 2022, showing consistent improvement.


COVID-19 Impact (2020-2021)
The pandemic caused the most ranking volatility across all states due to school closures and disrupted academic calendars.

What Does This Tell Us?

Access to schools is high.
But equity across states
still needs attention.

Malaysia has achieved near-universal literacy supported by a strong public education system. However, disparities between states — particularly between urban centres like Selangor and rural states like Sarawak — suggest that access alone does not guarantee equal outcomes.
