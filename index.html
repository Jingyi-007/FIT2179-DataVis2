Malaysia's Education Landscape

A closer look at the distribution of primary public schools across Malaysian states.

Distribution of Schools and Students

States with more schools generally have higher student enrolment, though population density also plays a role.

Public Schools Distribution by State (2022)

Darker colour indicates more schools. Sabah and Sarawak have the most.

Student Enrolment by State (2022)

Selangor has the highest enrolment followed by Johor and Sabah.

Public Schools Distribution by State & Level (2022)

Darker colour indicates more schools. States are sorted by total number of schools. Sarawak and Sabah have the most primary schools due to larger geographic coverage and rural communities.

Student Enrolment Trend (2017-2022)

Primary school enrolment has steadily increased while secondary enrolment dipped in 2018 before recovering. Post-secondary enrolment dropped sharply in 2020 likely due to COVID-19 disruptions.

Student Enrolment by State (2022)

Selangor has the highest total enrolment followed by Johor and Sabah. Primary school enrolment dominates across all states with post-secondary enrolment remaining relatively small.

School Completion Rates by State (2022)

Most Malaysian states achieve near or above 100% completion rates. W.P. Kuala Lumpur and W.P. Labuan show relatively lower primary completion rates. Rates above 100% occur due to cross-district enrolment patterns.

School Completion Rate Trend (2016-2022)

Overall completion rates have been improving across all levels. A notable dip in Secondary Upper completion rate in 2020 reflects the disruption caused by COVID-19 school closures.

Gender Gap in Primary School Completion (2022)

👧 Girls consistently outperform 👦 boys in primary school completion rates across all Malaysian states. The gap is most notable in W.P. Labuan and Pahang.

School Access vs Completion Rate (2022)

Each bubble represents a Malaysian state. Bubble size indicates total student enrolment. States with more schools generally maintain high completion rates. W.P. Kuala Lumpur and W.P. Labuan are notable outliers with lower completion despite urban location.
